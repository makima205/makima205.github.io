Bamboo research & learning center “Weaving The Future Of Khonoma”

Project Client

Urban Khetti

Project Category

Learning center

Location

Kohima, Nagaland

Status

Completed

Area

125 Sqm

For this competition we have designed a bamboo research & learning center at Kohima. In 1998, Khonoma village notified 2000 ha (20 sq. km) of its forest land as a nature conservation sanctuary, and banned logging and hunting. The existing structures were studied & we have found out that the structure mostly was made of the wood, mud & bamboo & now mostly the bricks made of bricks & concrete. Local natural material is still continued to be used for various purposes. The traditional naga house is made of timber, bamboo & thatch. The timber was used for structure elements, like beams etc. Bamboo for walls & other structure elements, thatch as a roofing material. While designing this we have used bamboo as a construction material, soil from landslides, using conventional resources such as solar, vortex turbines, water harvesting, use of alder tree wood, avoiding concrete. using locals to build, using local bamboos these are the factors contributing for less CO2 emission & makes the building energy efficient. This learning centre gives an opportunity to individuals to seek knowledge about bamboo, Bamboo specialists from all over world will come to guide locals, Locals will teach traditional bamboo & cane craft to tourists & trainees from all over globe. The Project can be made energy efficient through the key factors like the sustainable construction technology, using soil & stone from landslides, collecting water from roof & store in water reservoir, roof projections- protects from monsoon heavy rain as well as from harsh sun rays, steps are created to avoid the soil erosion, as we have used the bamboo as a structural material makes the design earthquake resistant & using nut bolt technique to replace any part of the structure at any time.
